[MASTER-04] P0 | Expert: 12 (Accessibility/WCAG)

  background (#1E2761):

      for progress bars and icons
RATIONALE: Affects readability for 4.5% of users with visual
  impairments. The most-viewed data points in the app (calorie
  target, remaining calories, water total) all use failing colors.

[MASTER-06] P0 | Experts: 5, 8, 16
FILE: src/utils/storage.ts:136-148
FINDING: No AsyncStorage key size monitoring. Any value of any
  size can be written without warning. Android CursorWindow 2MB
  limit is not enforced. Food cache (~1MB at 500 items) and
  coach history (grows linearly) can silently exceed the limit,
  causing an unrecoverable crash on Android.
RECOMMENDATION: Add JSON.stringify(value).length check in
  storageSet(). Warn at 1MB, block at 1.5MB with StorageError.
  Add data compaction for large values.
PERSPECTIVES:
  Expert 5 (CTO): CursorWindow crash is silent and unrecoverable.
  Expert 8 (Systems): Without monitoring, crash has no error msg.
  Expert 16 (QA): No automated test can verify the limit because
    the limit itself is not implemented.

DISSENT 1: Services tight coupling — P0 or P2?
  Expert 5 (CTO) says: P0 ship-blocker. The #1 architectural

  Expert 6 (SWE) says: (Implicit P2) No code quality concern

    abstraction for a feature that works.
  Tension: Expert 5 views architecture through v2 migration lens;
    Expert 6 views through v1.1 shipping lens. Both are right
    in their frame.
  Josh Decision Needed: Is DataRepository abstraction a v1.1
    ship-blocker or a v1.2 technical debt item? This depends
    on how soon v2 migration begins.

DISSENT 2: Log tab reorganization — P0 or P1?
  Expert 18 (End User) says: P0 ship-blocker. 20 buttons is
    fundamentally broken. Cannot ship this to App Store.
  Expert 13 (UX Researcher) says: P1. Functional but suboptimal.
    The buttons work; the hierarchy doesn't.
  Tension: End user experiences this emotionally (frustration);
    UX researcher evaluates it analytically (functional but
    inefficient).
  Josh Decision Needed: Is the current flat grid acceptable for
    a limited TestFlight beta, or must it be reorganized before
    any external users see it?

DISSENT 3: Pattern engine thresholds — spec or stricter?
  Expert 3 (Psychologist) says: Code uses 14-day minimum where
    spec says 7. Stricter is safer for avoiding unreliable
    correlations, but should be aligned with spec.
  Expert 9 (Data Scientist) says: 7 data points is too few for
    reliable group comparison. 14 is statistically appropriate.
  Tension: Spec compliance vs statistical rigor. Following the
    spec risks unreliable patterns; ignoring the spec risks audit
    inconsistency.
  Josh Decision Needed: Accept the stricter thresholds and update
    spec, or follow spec's 7-point minimum? The middle ground is
    to use 7 for counting patterns and 14 for comparison patterns.

DISSENT 4: Strength training — P1 or defer to v1.2?
  Expert 2 (CPT) says: P1. Strength logging is spec-required.
    Without set-by-set logging, the app cannot track volume or
    detect PRs — fundamental for strength athletes.
  Expert 18 (End User) says: Multi-activity logging (adding
    weights after cardio) is more important than detailed set
    tracking. Just let me log "30 min weights" in the same flow
    as my run.
  Tension: Expert 2 wants depth (full strength protocol); Expert
    18 wants breadth (log two activities simply).
  Josh Decision Needed: Full strength logger for v1.1, simplified
    multi-activity logging, or defer both to v1.2?

DISSENT 5: Tablet support — disable or implement?
  Expert 7 (iOS Dev) says: Set supportsTablet to false. iPhone-
    only for v1.1 is acceptable.
  Implicit market concern: Disabling iPad narrows addressable
    market. Some users prefer iPad for health tracking at home.
  Tension: Ship quality vs market reach.
  Josh Decision Needed: iPhone-only for v1.1 (clean, focused)
    or keep tablet enabled (wider reach, stretched UI)?
