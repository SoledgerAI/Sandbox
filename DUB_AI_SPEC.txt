SECTION 1: PRODUCT IDENTITY
================================================================================

Name: DUB_AI Tracker

Thesis: "I want to get better" app. Not social media. No ego. No leaderboards.
No sharing. No badges. Private, data-driven accountability. The AI coach
recognizes patterns and proactively surfaces insights. Every feature exists to
help the user become healthier.

Tone: Clinical expertise delivered with warmth. A world-class nutritionist and
personal trainer who lives in your pocket.

North Star Metric: Daily active logging consistency (7-day rolling average of
days with at least one tag logged).

SECTION 2: TECH STACK
================================================================================

Framework: React Native + Expo (managed workflow with expo-dev-client)
Navigation: Expo Router (file-based routing under app/ directory)
Storage: AsyncStorage (v1, no backend yet)
  CRITICAL: Set AsyncStorage_db_size_in_MB=50 in android/gradle.properties.
  Default 6MB WILL be exceeded with food log history.
  Per-value read limit is 2MB (Android CursorWindow constraint).
  Plan v2 migration to SQLite (expo-sqlite) or WatermelonDB.
Language: TypeScript strict mode, no any types, strict null checks enabled
HealthKit (iOS): react-native-health
HealthKit (Android): react-native-health-connect
Dev Client: expo-dev-client (NOT Expo Go -- HealthKit native modules require
  custom dev client builds)
Deployment: EAS Build for App Store / Google Play
AI Model: claude-sonnet-4-20250514 via Anthropic Messages API
  Endpoint: https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages
  Auth: x-api-key header with user-provided key (stored encrypted in
  AsyncStorage, never transmitted except to Anthropic)
  Max tokens per Coach response: 1024
  Temperature: 0.7 (Coach persona)

SECTION 4: CRITICAL DESIGN RULES
================================================================================

Rule 1 -- TIMESTAMPS ON EVERYTHING
Every logged data point stores a full ISO 8601 timestamp (YYYY-MM-DDTHH:mm:ss.sssZ)
in UTC. Display in user local time. Never discard time component. This enables
time-of-day correlation analysis (e.g., "your mood scores 15% higher on days you
exercise before 9 AM").

Rule 2 -- NO SOCIAL FEATURES
No sharing. No friends. No followers. No feed. No comparing to others. No
community features. No public profiles. This is a private tool. Period.

Rule 3 -- NO GAMIFICATION EGO
No leaderboards. No badges. No achievements for achievements' sake. No "streaks"
that punish missing a day (streak counter exists as data, but missing a day does
NOT trigger shame). Celebrations are data-driven and subtle (gold shimmer
animation, not confetti cannons).

Rule 4 -- ELECTIVE TAGS ONLY
Users choose which health dimensions to track during onboarding. No tag is
mandatory. No "you should also track X" nudges. The app respects what the user
chooses to monitor. Tags can be added/removed anytime in Settings.

Rule 5 -- NO SHAME-BASED NOTIFICATIONS
Every notification surfaces data and asks questions. Never uses language like
"you missed," "you failed," "you're behind," "don't give up." Instead: "Your
water intake today is 32oz. Your average is 64oz. Want to log more?" Data, not
guilt.

SECTION 5: NAVIGATION
================================================================================

Bottom tab bar with 5 tabs. Always visible. Gold (#D4A843) active icon, gray
(#B0B0B0) inactive icons. No labels on inactive tabs (icon only). Active tab
shows label below icon.

Tab 1 -- Dashboard (home icon)
  Daily overview. Score ring. Recovery score. BMR/TDEE/calorie summary. Net
  calories. Streak counter. Enabled tag cards with sparklines. Cards are
  reorderable via long-press drag.

Tab 2 -- Log (plus-circle icon)
  Quick logging hub. Shows enabled tags as cards. Tap to log. Recent entries
  list below. Barcode scanner button prominent at top for food logging.

Tab 3 -- Coach (message-circle icon)
  AI Coach chat interface. Claude-powered. Suggested prompts at top. Full
  conversation history. Data context injected per message.

Tab 4 -- Trends (bar-chart icon)
  53 chart types. Time range selector (7d/30d/90d/6mo/1yr/all). Year-over-year
  overlay available after 12+ months of data. Chart categories map to enabled
  tags.

Tab 5 -- Settings (gear icon)
  Profile, engagement tier, tag management, device connections, notification
  preferences, data export, API key management, health report generation, theme
  (future), units (imperial/metric), about/legal.

SECTION 8: ONBOARDING FLOW
================================================================================

Per Expert 5 UX Audit (Severity: HIGH): Original 6-step flow had an estimated
50-55% completion rate due to weight entry anxiety, calorie complexity, and
tier decision paralysis. Redesigned to reduce friction while preserving all
safety requirements from Experts 1, 3, and 4.

4 steps. Progress bar at top. Back button on steps 2-4. Skip button on steps
3-4. All data saved to AsyncStorage on each step completion.

Step 1 -- Profile and Consent
  Section A -- Privacy Consent (NOT skippable):
    "DUB_AI collects health and wellness data that you choose to log. This
    data is stored on your device. When you use the AI Coach, your data is
    transmitted to a third-party AI service (Anthropic) for processing.
    [Link: Read our full Privacy Policy]
    [Link: Read our Terms of Service]
    [Checkbox] I consent to the collection and processing of my health
      data as described in the Privacy Policy.
    [Checkbox] I understand that Coach messages are processed by a
      third-party AI service.
    [Checkbox] I am 18 years of age or older."
    All three checkboxes required to proceed. Per Expert 3 Audit.
    Storage: dub.settings.consent_date, dub.settings.consent_version

  Section B -- Basic Profile:
    Fields: Name (string), Date of Birth (date picker), Unit Preference
    (Imperial/Metric).
    Validation: Age must be 18+. If under 18: "DUB_AI Tracker is designed
    for adults 18 and older. Please check back when you turn 18." Block.
    DEFERRED TO DASHBOARD: Height, Weight, and Sex are NOT collected here.
    They are prompted on first Dashboard visit: "To calculate your nutrition
    targets, we need a few more details." This gives the user a reason for
    providing sensitive data (weight) rather than demanding it cold.
    Per Expert 5 Audit: deferring weight reduces Step 1 drop-off by ~15%.
  Storage: dub.profile (partial -- name, dob, units)

Step 2 -- Goal Selection (Simplified)
  Three plain-language choices (no calorie math shown):
    "I want to lose weight" (maps to LOSE goal_direction)
    "I want to gain weight or build muscle" (maps to GAIN goal_direction)
    "I want to maintain my weight" (maps to MAINTAIN goal_direction)
  Plus: "I'm not sure yet / Skip" (maps to MAINTAIN as safe default)

  Rate selection, target weight, deficit/surplus details, and gain type
  (lean/standard/aggressive) are DEFERRED to Settings > Profile after the
  user has context from using the app. Defaults applied silently:
    LOSE: 1.0 lb/wk moderate deficit (can adjust later)
    GAIN: Standard Bulk 500 cal surplus (can adjust later)
    MAINTAIN: TDEE neutral
  Per Expert 5 Audit: simplifying from 5+ options to 3 plain choices reduces
  decision paralysis and Step 2 drop-off.

  All safety floors (1,200/1,500 cal minimum), gain-specific Coach behavior,
  body recomp logic, and NHLBI source citations from previous spec versions
  remain in effect -- they are enforced in the calorie engine and Settings
  UI, not in onboarding.
  Storage: dub.profile (goal_direction)

Step 3 -- Engagement Tier and Tags (Skippable)
  TIER NAMES REVISED per Expert 5 Audit (Severity: MEDIUM). Names describe
  the APPROACH, not the user's character:
    Precision (100%): Every calorie counted. Maximum accountability. For
      competitive athletes and people who thrive on exact tracking.
    Structured (90/10): 90% on plan with some flexibility. Weekly adherence
      tracking. For serious goal-chasers.
    Balanced (85/15) [DEFAULT]: 5-day rolling averages over daily perfection.
      Accountability without obsession.
    Flexible (80/20): Weekly and monthly trends, not daily numbers. For
      sustainable long-term habits.
    Mindful (Maintain): Awareness without restriction. Weight stability focus.

  TAG SELECTION (two-section grid per Expert 5 Audit, Severity: HIGH):
    Section 1 -- "Health and Fitness" (non-sensitive):
      Hydration, Nutrition, Fitness, Strength, Body, Sleep, Recovery,
      Supplements, Health Markers.
      Pre-selected based on tier (same defaults as before, using new names).

    Section 2 -- "Personal and Private" (sensitive):
      Introduced with: "These tags track sensitive health data. They are
      completely private, stored only on your device, and optional."
      Tags: Mental Wellness, Substances, Sexual Activity, Digestive Health,
      Personal Care, Women's Health, Injury/Pain, Custom.
      NONE pre-selected regardless of tier. Always require explicit opt-in.
      Per Expert 5 Audit: sensitive tags should never be pre-selected.

  SHOULDER-SURFING PROTECTION: For sensitive tag screens (Sexual Activity,
  Substances, Therapy notes), add a quick-hide gesture (triple-tap to blank
  the screen). Add a "Privacy Screen" toggle in Settings that blurs sensitive
  tag content when the app enters the recent apps/task switcher view.

  If skipped: Balanced tier selected, default Health and Fitness tags enabled,
  no sensitive tags enabled. User can customize anytime in Settings.
  Storage: dub.tier, dub.tags.enabled

Step 4 -- Devices and Notifications (Skippable)
  Combined into single step with two sections:
    Section A -- Device Connections:
      Apple Health (iOS) / Google Health Connect (Android) -- permission request
      Strava -- OAuth flow
      Garmin -- "Coming Soon" stub
      Oura -- "Coming Soon" stub
    Section B -- Notification Preferences:
      Enable push notifications (system permission request)
      Notification cadence preview based on selected tier
      Defaults applied silently (can customize in Settings later)
  Storage: dub.devices.*, dub.settings

DEFERRED DATA COLLECTION (triggered on first Dashboard visit):
  After onboarding completes, the first Dashboard visit shows a single card:
  "To calculate your nutrition targets and track progress, we need:"
    Biological Sex (Male/Female/Prefer Not to Say)
    Height (ft/in or cm)
    Current Weight (lbs or kg)
    Activity Level (Sedentary/Lightly Active/Moderately Active/Very Active/
      Extremely Active)
  "Why do we need this?" expandable explanation for each field.
  Once provided, BMR/TDEE compute and Dashboard populates fully.
  Until provided, Dashboard shows: streaks, enabled tag cards, Coach access,
  but calorie summary shows "Complete your profile to see calorie targets."
  Storage: dub.profile (completed with sex, height, weight, activity_level)

On completion: Set dub.onboarding.complete = true, dub.onboarding.date = now.
Navigate to Dashboard (which triggers deferred data collection on first visit).

SECTION 9: ENGAGEMENT TIER SYSTEM -- FIVE TIERS
================================================================================

Each tier defines a behavioral contract between the user and the AI Coach.
Tiers affect: Coach tone, deviation flagging, end-of-day questionnaire focus,
daily score weighting, product recommendations, notification cadence, and
reporting detail.

Tier definitions are stored in constants/tiers.ts as a configurable data
structure, not hardcoded throughout the app.

TIER 1: PRECISION (100%) [formerly Draconian]
  Adherence Target: 100% of planned calories and macros daily
  Flex Meals: 0 per month
  Coach Tone: Direct, clinical, precise. No softening. "You are 340 calories
    over your target. That is a 9.7% deviation. Your weekly average is now
    2,180 vs your 2,000 target."
  Deviation Flagging: Batch feedback at 3 daily checkpoints (post-lunch
    summary, post-dinner summary, end-of-day). NEVER flag after a single
    meal in isolation -- one meal does not define a day. This preserves
    Precision tier rigor while preventing per-meal punishment loops that
    can reinforce disordered eating. Per Expert 5 Audit (Severity: MEDIUM)
    and Expert 4 eating disorder risk finding.
  End-of-Day Focus: Full macro breakdown vs targets. Every tag reviewed.
    Deviations itemized with exact numbers.
  Daily Score Weighting: Calorie accuracy 40%, Macro accuracy 25%, Tag
    completion 20%, Consistency 15%
  Notification Cadence: 6-8 per day (hydration + meal reminders + macro checks
    + EOD)
  Product Recommendations: Performance-focused (precision scales, macro-counted
    meal delivery, competition prep supplements)

TIER 2: STRUCTURED (90/10) [formerly Disciplined]
  Adherence Target: 90% of days fully on plan per month
  Flex Meals: Approximately 3 per month (roughly 1 per 10 days)
  Coach Tone: Encouraging but data-forward. "Good day overall. You hit 92% of
    your protein target. One area to watch: your afternoon snack pushed you 180
    over on carbs."
  Deviation Flagging: Flags deviations over 10% from daily targets. Tracks
    weekly adherence percentage. Flex meals logged but not flagged if within
    monthly allowance.
  End-of-Day Focus: Daily adherence score. Weekly adherence percentage.
    Highlights wins and one improvement area.
  Daily Score Weighting: Calorie accuracy 35%, Macro accuracy 20%, Tag
    completion 25%, Consistency 20%
  Notification Cadence: 3-5 per day (morning + meal windows + EOD)
  Product Recommendations: Goal-oriented (meal prep containers, protein
    supplements, fitness trackers)

TIER 3: BALANCED (85/15) [formerly Committed]
  Adherence Target: 85% of days on plan per month
  Flex Meals: More flexible -- Coach tracks 5-day rolling average
  Coach Tone: Supportive, trend-focused. "Your 5-day rolling average is 1,920
    calories against your 2,000 target. Right in the zone. Today was a little
    high but the trend looks great."
  Deviation Flagging: Flags only when 5-day rolling average deviates more than
    15% from targets. Single-day spikes noted without alarm.
  End-of-Day Focus: Rolling averages. Trend direction (improving, stable,
    declining). Top 2 positive patterns.
  Daily Score Weighting: Trend alignment 35%, Tag completion 25%, Consistency
    25%, Calorie accuracy 15%
  Notification Cadence: 3-5 per day
  Product Recommendations: Balanced (general wellness, kitchen tools, wearables)

TIER 4: FLEXIBLE (80/20) [formerly Lifestyle]
  Adherence Target: 80% of days with some logging activity
  Flex Meals: Not tracked individually -- weekly and monthly trends only
  Coach Tone: Relaxed, encouraging, big-picture. "This week you averaged 2,100
    calories. Your monthly trend is down 50 from last month. Heading the right
    direction."
  Deviation Flagging: Only flags sustained trends (7+ day sliding window
    showing consistent overshoot or undershoot of more than 20%).
  End-of-Day Focus: Quick summary. "You logged 3 meals and hit your water
    goal. Nice work." Minimal detail unless user asks.
  Daily Score Weighting: Logging consistency 40%, Weekly trend 30%, Tag
    completion 20%, Calorie accuracy 10%
  Notification Cadence: 1-3 per day (morning + EOD only)
  Product Recommendations: Lifestyle (kitchen gadgets, water bottles, books,
    outdoor gear)

TIER 5: MINDFUL (MAINTAIN) [formerly Calorie Conscious]
  Adherence Target: TDEE equals daily calorie target. No deficit, no surplus.
  Flex Meals: N/A -- maintenance mode
  Coach Tone: Observational, low-pressure. "Weight stable at 175 for the past
    14 days. Right in your 3 lb band. Calorie average this week: 2,310 vs
    your 2,300 TDEE."
  Deviation Flagging: Flags only when weight exits the plus/minus 3 lb band
    from target weight for 3+ consecutive weigh-ins. Calorie flags only for
    sustained plus/minus 15% TDEE deviation over 7+ days.
  End-of-Day Focus: Weight trend. Calorie average vs TDEE. Quick check.
  Daily Score Weighting: Weight stability 40%, Calorie TDEE alignment 30%,
    Logging consistency 20%, Tag completion 10%
  Notification Cadence: 1-3 per day
  Product Recommendations: Maintenance-focused (body composition scales, meal
    variety, wellness subscriptions)

Tier is selectable during onboarding (Step 3) and changeable anytime in
Settings. Changing tier takes effect immediately. Coach acknowledges the change
in next interaction.

CROSS-TIER SAFETY: EATING DISORDER RISK GUARDRAILS
Per Expert 4 AI/ML Audit (Severity: HIGH). Eating disorder risk is the most
serious health safety concern in a calorie tracking app. Real-time calorie
deviation flagging (especially the Precision tier) can reinforce restrictive
behavior in at-risk individuals. These guardrails apply to ALL tiers:

  1. Calorie floor enforcement (from Expert 1): The app NEVER sets a target
     below 1,200 cal (women) or 1,500 cal (men). This is enforced in the
     calorie engine, not just the UI.

  2. Sustained low intake alert: If the user's logged calorie intake falls
     below 1,200 (women) or 1,500 (men) for 3+ consecutive days, the Coach
     generates a proactive message: "Your calorie intake has been consistently
     below [threshold] for [N] days. This level may not support your health
     and wellbeing. Consider discussing your nutrition goals with a healthcare
     provider." This overrides tier-specific tone -- even Precision Coach
     delivers this message with care.

  3. Healthy BMI + weight loss flag: If the user's current weight places them
     at or below a BMI of 18.5 (underweight) or within the normal BMI range
     (18.5-24.9) AND they have an active weight loss goal, the Coach flags:
     "Your current weight is within (or below) the healthy BMI range for your
     height. Continued weight loss at this weight should be discussed with a
     healthcare provider." This flag appears during onboarding Step 2 AND
     as a Coach proactive insight if weight drops into this range.

  4. Coach NEVER celebrates extreme restriction: In ANY tier, if daily
     calorie intake is below 1,000, the Coach NEVER responds with positive
     reinforcement. No "great discipline," no "impressive willpower," no
     celebration of restriction. The Coach surfaces the data factually and
     includes the healthcare provider recommendation.

  5. System prompt hard rule: Add to coach_system_prompt.ts:
     "SAFETY RULE: If the user's logged intake is consistently below minimum
     safe thresholds, prioritize health safety over tier adherence. A user
     who is 'on plan' at 800 calories is NOT succeeding -- they need a
     healthcare provider. Never reinforce extreme caloric restriction."

-- FOOD INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM --
  8 methods of food entry:
  1. Barcode Scan: Camera-based UPC scan. Lookup waterfall: FatSecret API >
     Open Food Facts > USDA > manual entry prompt.
  2. Restaurant Search: Search by restaurant name + menu item. Source:
     FatSecret restaurant data + Nutritionix (if licensed).
  3. NLP Text Entry: Type natural language ("2 eggs, toast with butter, coffee
     with cream"). Parse with AI Coach or local NLP rules to structured entry.
  4. Photo + AI: Take photo of meal. Send to Claude API with prompt to estimate
     food items and portions. Returns structured food entry for user
     confirmation. User ALWAYS confirms/edits before saving.
     ACCURACY EXPECTATIONS (Per Expert 4 Audit, Severity: MEDIUM):
       Calorie estimates from photos typically have +/- 30-50% error range.
       Portion size perception is affected by plate size, camera angle, food
       density, and whether items are obscured. Mixed dishes (casseroles,
       stews, bowls) are particularly inaccurate.
     Photo Estimation Prompt (store in ai/photo_prompt.ts):
       "Analyze this meal photo. For each visible food item:
       1. Identify the food item
       2. Estimate the portion size in common units (cups, oz, pieces)
       3. Provide nutrition estimates (calories, protein, carbs, fat)
       4. Provide a confidence level (high/medium/low) for each estimate
       5. Flag items that are difficult to estimate accurately
       Return as JSON: { items: [{ name, portion, calories, protein_g,
       carbs_g, fat_g, confidence, note }], total_calories, disclaimer }"
     UI: Display each estimated item as an EDITABLE card with confidence
       indicator (green=high, gold=medium, red=low). User can adjust any
       value before saving. Display disclaimer: "Photo estimates may vary
       +/- 30%. Please review and adjust portions before saving."
     Cost per photo: ~$0.004-0.01 (acceptable for consumer use).
  5. Recipe Builder: Enter ingredients and quantities, app computes total
     nutrition per serving. Save as reusable template.
  6. Quick Log: Calorie-only entry with optional protein. For when user knows
     approximate calories but not full nutrition.
  7. Favorites: Re-log previously saved favorite foods with one tap.
  8. Meal Templates: Re-log saved meal combinations (e.g., "My usual breakfast").

-- SERVING SIZE MATH ENGINE --
  Multiplier range: 0.25x through 10x in 0.25 increments, plus custom decimal.
  ALL nutrition values scale linearly with serving multiplier.
  Display: Show base serving size AND adjusted values. E.g., "1 cup (base) x
    1.5 = 1.5 cups"
  Rounding: Round to whole numbers for display (e.g., 147 cal, not 146.7).
  Precision: Retain full decimal precision in stored data. Round only at display.

  Unit System: The engine must handle real-world serving size entry, not just
  multipliers. Users think in cups, tablespoons, fluid ounces, pieces, slices,
  and grams interchangeably. The system must convert between them logically.

  Supported Serving Units:
    Volume: cup, tablespoon (tbsp), teaspoon (tsp), fluid ounce (fl oz),
      milliliter (ml), liter (L)
    Weight: gram (g), ounce (oz), pound (lb), kilogram (kg)
    Count: piece, slice, patty, fillet, scoop, packet, container, each

  Volume-to-Weight Conversion:
    Problem: 1 cup of peanut butter does not weigh the same as 1 cup of
    spinach. Volume-to-weight conversion requires food-specific density.
    Solution: USDA FoodData Central provides gram weight per common measure
    (e.g., "1 cup" = 258g for whole milk, "1 cup" = 30g for raw spinach).
    When a food is loaded from any API source, store the gram_weight_per_unit
    mapping for each available serving description. This enables:
      - User enters "2 cups" of milk > system knows 2 x 258g = 516g > scales
        nutrition from per-100g base data
      - User enters "3 tbsp" of olive oil > system knows 3 x 13.5g = 40.5g
      - User enters "6 oz" of chicken breast > direct weight, no density needed

    Fallback for missing density data: If a food has no gram-per-unit mapping
    for the selected unit, prompt the user: "We don't have a conversion for
    [food] in [unit]. Can you enter the weight in grams or ounces instead?"
    Do NOT guess density. Wrong density = wrong calories = broken trust.

  Standard Volume Conversions (always accurate, food-independent):
    1 cup = 16 tablespoons = 48 teaspoons = 8 fl oz = 236.588 ml
    1 tablespoon = 3 teaspoons = 0.5 fl oz = 14.787 ml
    1 fl oz = 29.574 ml
    1 liter = 1000 ml = 33.814 fl oz = 4.227 cups

  Standard Weight Conversions (always accurate, food-independent):
    1 oz = 28.3495 g
    1 lb = 453.592 g = 16 oz
    1 kg = 1000 g = 2.20462 lbs

  UI Flow: When user selects a food, display available serving options from
  the API data (e.g., "1 cup (258g)", "1 tbsp (15g)", "100g", "1 container
  (245g)"). User picks a serving unit, then adjusts quantity. System computes
  gram weight, scales nutrition. User always sees both the friendly unit
  ("1.5 cups") and the computed weight ("387g") for transparency.

  Environmental and Biometric Fields (optional per activity, significantly
  improve calorie accuracy when available):

    elevation_gain_ft: Total elevation gained during activity (feet or meters
      per unit preference). Impacts effort significantly -- a 3-mile run with
      1,500 ft gain is vastly different from flat. If elevation gain is logged,
      apply elevation calorie adjustment: add approximately 3-5% per 500 ft of
      gain to base MET calorie burn. This is an approximation; precise
      adjustment depends on grade, speed, and body weight. [VERIFY -- validate
      percentage adjustment against published research on graded exercise
      energy cost. Candidate source: Minetti AE, et al., J Appl Physiol,
      2002;93(3):1039-1046. DOI: 10.1152/japplphysiol.01177.2001]

    elevation_loss_ft: Total elevation lost (descending). Eccentric load on
      descents is less calorie-intensive than climbing but still meaningful.
      Track for completeness and Coach insight.

    altitude_ft: Altitude at which exercise was performed (feet or meters).
      Exercising at altitude (e.g., Lake Tahoe at ~6,225 ft vs Monterey, CA
      at sea level) increases energy expenditure due to reduced oxygen
      availability and increased ventilatory demand. General guideline: at
      altitudes above 4,000 ft, energy expenditure may increase 5-15%
      depending on acclimatization status.
      Source: Butterfield GE. Nutrient requirements at high altitude. Clin
        Sports Med. 1999;18(3):607-621.
        DOI: 10.1016/S0278-5919(05)70171-X [VERIFY DOI]
      Implementation: If altitude > 4,000 ft and user is not marked as
      altitude-acclimated (acclimation flag in profile, default: not
      acclimated), apply a configurable altitude adjustment factor to calorie
      burn. Default: 10% increase above 4,000 ft. Store as configurable
      constant ALTITUDE_CALORIE_ADJUSTMENT_FACTOR = 0.10 and
      ALTITUDE_THRESHOLD_FT = 4000 in constants/formulas.ts.
      Coach insight: "You exercised at 7,200 ft today. Altitude increases
      energy demand -- your effective calorie burn is estimated ~10% higher
      than at sea level."

    temperature_f: Ambient temperature during exercise (Fahrenheit or Celsius
      per unit preference). Extreme heat and cold both increase energy
      expenditure (thermoregulation cost). At temperatures above 90F or below
      40F, metabolic cost increases.
      Implementation: Track as data for Coach correlation analysis. Do NOT
      auto-adjust calorie burn for temperature (too many variables --
      clothing, humidity, wind, acclimatization). Instead, Coach surfaces
      patterns: "Your average pace drops 45 sec/mile on days above 85F.
      Consider earlier start times in summer."
      Weather integration (Phase 18) can auto-populate temperature if user
      grants location access.

    avg_heart_rate_bpm: Average heart rate during activity (manual entry or
      device sync from Apple Health / Garmin / Oura). Heart rate is the best
      real-time proxy for actual energy expenditure.
      Future enhancement (v2): When heart rate data is available, offer
      HR-based calorie estimation as alternative to MET-based:
        Calories/min (men) = (-55.0969 + 0.6309 x HR + 0.1988 x wt_kg +
          0.2017 x age) / 4.184
        Calories/min (women) = (-20.4022 + 0.4472 x HR + 0.1263 x wt_kg +
          0.0740 x age) / 4.184
        Source: Keytel LR, et al., J Sports Sci, 2005;23(3):289-297.
          DOI: 10.1080/02640410470001730089 [VERIFY DOI]
      V1: Store heart rate data for Coach analysis and correlation. Display
      alongside MET-based calorie estimate. Do not replace MET calculation
      in v1.

    max_heart_rate_bpm: Peak heart rate during activity (manual or device).

    heart_rate_zones: If device provides zone data (Zone 1-5 with time in

-- RECOVERY SCORE --
  DUB_AI Proprietary Recovery Score v1.0
  Composite weighted score, scale 0-100.

  Components and Weights (stored as CONFIGURABLE CONSTANTS in
  constants/formulas.ts, not hardcoded in computation logic):
    Sleep Quality: 25% (user-reported 1-5 scale, normalized to 0-100)
    Sleep Duration: 20% (scored against 7-9 hour optimal range per NSF
      recommendation. Below 6 hours = 0-30, 6-7 = 30-60, 7-9 = 60-100,
      above 9 = 80-90 with diminishing return)
      Source: National Sleep Foundation, Sleep Duration Recommendations.
      Hirshkowitz M, et al., Sleep Health. 2015;1(1):40-43.
      DOI: 10.1016/j.sleh.2014.12.010
    HRV: 20% (scored against personal baseline. Above 7-day average = higher
      score, below = lower. No absolute threshold -- individual baseline only.)
    Resting HR: 15% (scored against personal baseline. Lower than 7-day average
      = higher score. No absolute threshold.)
    Training Load: 15% (inversely scored. Higher training load yesterday =
      lower recovery today. Based on estimated calorie burn from logged
      workouts. Scored against 7-day average load.)
    Alcohol: 5% (penalty. 0 drinks = 100, 1 drink = 80, 2 drinks = 50,
      3+ drinks = 20. Simple step function.)

  Computation: Sum(component_score x component_weight) for all available
  components. If a component has no data (e.g., user does not track HRV),
  redistribute its weight proportionally across available components. Minimum
  3 components required to display score; otherwise show "Insufficient Data."

  Display: Large circular gauge on Dashboard. Color-coded: 80+ green (success),
  50-79 gold (warning), below 50 red (danger).

FORMULA 1: BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) -- Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

  Men:    BMR = (9.99 x weight_kg) + (6.25 x height_cm) - (4.92 x age_years) + 5
  Women:  BMR = (9.99 x weight_kg) + (6.25 x height_cm) - (4.92 x age_years) - 161

  CRITICAL: Use PRECISE coefficients 9.99 and 4.92. NOT rounded 10 and 5.
  The original paper uses 9.99 and 4.92.
  DEVELOPER NOTE: Most clinical calculators (Medscape, QxMD) round to 10/5.
  The practical difference is typically less than 5 kcal/day for most users.
  We use the original paper's precise coefficients for source fidelity.
  If cross-referencing against other tools, expect minor discrepancies.
  Per Expert 1 Clinical Audit verification.

  Source: Mifflin MD, St Jeor ST, Hill LA, Scott BJ, Daugherty SA, Koh YO.
    A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy
    individuals. Am J Clin Nutr. 1990;51(2):241-247.
    DOI: 10.1093/ajcn/51.2.241

  Implementation Notes:
    - If user selects "Prefer Not to Say" for sex, use average of male and
      female formulas: ((male_result + female_result) / 2)
    - Weight in kg: if user enters lbs, convert (lbs / 2.20462)
    - Height in cm: if user enters ft/in, convert (inches_total x 2.54)
    - Age computed from date of birth, not user-entered age (avoids staleness)
    - Recalculate daily (age may change; weight updates when logged)


FORMULA 2: TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

  TDEE = BMR x Activity Multiplier

  Activity Multiplier Levels:
    Sedentary (little or no exercise): 1.2
    Lightly Active (light exercise 1-3 days/week): 1.375
    Moderately Active (moderate exercise 3-5 days/week): 1.55
    Very Active (hard exercise 6-7 days/week): 1.725
    Extremely Active (very hard exercise + physical job): 1.9

  Source: FAO/WHO/UNU. Energy and protein requirements. Report of a joint
    FAO/WHO/UNU Expert Consultation. WHO Technical Report Series 724. Geneva:
    World Health Organization; 1985.

  Implementation Notes:
    - User selects activity level during onboarding (Step 1 profile)
    - Can be changed in Settings
    - Future enhancement (Phase 2+): Auto-adjust activity multiplier based on
      logged exercise data (weekly average exercise minutes mapped to levels)
    - TDEE reconciliation: Compare predicted weight change (from cal in - cal
      out / 3500) to actual weight change weekly. If discrepancy exceeds 0.5
      lbs per week for 3+ consecutive weeks, Coach suggests TDEE adjustment.


FORMULA 3: MET-Based Calorie Burn

  Calories Burned = MET x weight_kg x duration_hours

  This is the standard application formula for Compendium MET values.

  Source for MET values: Herrmann SD, Willis EA, Ainsworth BE, Barreira TV,
    Hastert M, Kang M, Vasquez E, Schuna JM Jr. 2024 Adult Compendium of
    Physical Activities: A third update of the codes and MET values.
    J Sport Health Sci. 2024;13(1):6-12.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.jshs.2023.10.010 (corrected per Expert 1 Audit; PMID 38242596)
    Database URL: https://pacompendium.com

  The 2024 Compendium contains 1,114 activities with 5-digit activity codes.
  82% of MET values are based on measured energy expenditure data.

  Implementation:
    - Store MET values in data/met_compendium.json with Compendium 5-digit
      codes as keys.
    - For activities with multiple intensity levels (e.g., cycling at different
      speeds), store all variants and let user select intensity.
    - Example entries (verify all codes and values against pacompendium.com
      before build):
        Walking 3.0 mph, level surface: Code 17190, MET 3.5
        Running 6.0 mph (10 min/mile): Code 12050, MET 9.8
        Cycling 12-13.9 mph, moderate: Code 01040, MET 8.0
        Swimming laps, freestyle, moderate: Code 18260, MET 7.0
        Resistance training, moderate: Code 02054, MET 3.5 [VERIFY]
        Sexual activity, general, moderate: Code 14020, MET 3.0
          (See Section 10 Sexual Activity for full intensity table:
          Code 14010 MET 5.8 vigorous, Code 14020 MET 3.0 moderate,
          Code 14030 MET 1.8 light. Default: moderate.)
        Yoga, Hatha: Code 02172, MET 2.5
        Pickleball: [VERIFY code and MET in 2024 edition]
      [VERIFY ALL VALUES AGAINST 2024 COMPENDIUM BEFORE DATA FILE CREATION]


FORMULA 4: Weight Change Estimation

  Estimated Weekly Weight Change (lbs) = (Weekly Cal In - Weekly Cal Out) / 3500

  STATUS: KNOWN LIMITATION. The 3,500-calorie rule is an oversimplification.

  The static model assumes 1 lb of fat = 3,500 calories, which does not account
  for metabolic adaptation, changes in lean mass, water retention, glycogen
  fluctuations, or the dynamic nature of energy balance.

  Source acknowledging limitation: Hall KD, Chow CC. Estimating changes of
    free-living energy intake and physical activity from body weight changes.
    Int J Obes. 2013;37(12):1614. (Letter)
    Also: Hall KD, et al. Quantification of the effect of energy imbalance on
    bodyweight. Lancet. 2011;378(9793):826-837.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(11)60812-X

  Implementation:
    - Use as STARTING ESTIMATE ONLY for calorie target computation
    - MANDATORY: Weekly TDEE reconciliation. Compare predicted vs actual weight
      change. Adjust TDEE estimate if discrepancy persists for 3+ weeks.
    - USER DISCLAIMER (displayed on any screen showing weight projections):
      "Weight projections use a simplified model. Actual weight change depends
      on many factors including metabolism, water retention, and body
      composition changes. Weekly check-ins help us refine your targets."


FORMULA 5: 1RM Estimation (Brzycki)

  1RM = weight x (36 / (37 - reps))

  Valid for reps 1-10 only. Do not estimate from sets of more than 10 reps
  (formula becomes unreliable at higher rep ranges).

  Source: Brzycki M. Strength Testing -- Predicting a One-Rep Max from
    Reps-to-Fatigue. JOHPERD. 1993;64(1):88-90.
    DOI: 10.1080/07303084.1993.10606684

  Weights stored as configurable constants:

  2. Food-sleep correlations: "On days you consume more than 2,000mg sodium,
     your sleep quality averages 2.3 vs 3.5 on days below 1,500mg" (requires
     nutrition + sleep tags, 7+ days)
  3. Substance-performance: "Your workout volume is 22% higher on days
     following zero alcohol vs days following 2+ drinks" (requires substance +
     strength/fitness, 7+ days)
  4. Hydration-mood: "Days with 64+ oz water correlate with mood scores of
     3.8 vs 2.9 on days below 32 oz" (7+ days)
  5. Consistency-weight: "During weeks with 5+ logging days, your weight trend
     moves toward goal at 0.8 lbs/week vs 0.2 lbs/week in weeks with fewer
     than 3 logging days" (4+ weeks)
  6. Cycle-specific patterns (if enabled): Correlations across cycle phases
  7. Ingredient patterns: "You've logged foods with [flagged ingredient] X
     times this week" (3+ times/week)

Display: Pattern cards on Dashboard (collapsible) and available in Coach context.
Each pattern shows: observation, data range, sample sizes, correlation note.
Patterns do NOT claim causation. Language: "correlates with," "on days when,"
"your data shows" -- never "causes" or "because."


-- ADAPTIVE NOTIFICATION ARCHITECTURE --

End-of-Day Questionnaire Timing:
  Primary: ONE HOUR before observed bedtime.
  Observed bedtime = 7-day rolling average of sleep.bedtime logs.
  Fallback: 9:00 PM local time (if fewer than 3 bedtime data points).
  Never before 6 PM. Never after 11 PM.
  User can override in Settings with manual time.

Notification Cadence by Tier:
  Precision: 6-8 per day (morning brief, pre-meal reminders, macro check-ins,
    hydration, workout reminder, EOD)
  Structured: 3-5 per day (morning, midday check, hydration, EOD)
  Balanced: 3-5 per day (morning, hydration, EOD)
  Flexible: 1-3 per day (morning, EOD)
  Mindful: 1-3 per day (morning, EOD)
  Customize: All off. User builds from scratch, toggling individual
    notification types.

ALL notifications follow Rule 5 (No Shame). Every notification surfaces data
and asks a question. Examples:
  "Good morning! Yesterday you hit 87% of your protein target. Aiming for
   100% today?"
  "You've had 24oz of water today. Your goal is 80oz. Want to log more?"
  "It's 7 PM and you haven't logged dinner. Want to log now?"

Never: "You forgot to log dinner!" or "You missed your water goal again."


-- END-OF-DAY QUESTIONNAIRE --

Trigger: Adaptive timing (see above).
Format: Swipeable cards.
Smart: Only shows tags that have no data for today. If user already logged
  water, food, workout, mood -- those cards don't appear.

CARD MANAGEMENT (Per Expert 5 Audit, Severity: MEDIUM):
  Mobile questionnaire fatigue begins at 5-7 items. A Precision user with 15
  tags who logged 3 would face 12 cards -- unacceptable. Rules:

  1. Cap at 7 cards maximum. If more than 7 tags are unlogged, group related
     tags into combined cards:
       Mood + Stress + Gratitude → single "Mental Wellness" card with sub-inputs
       Personal Care items → single checklist card
       Supplements (vitamins + medications + supplements) → single checklist card
  2. Priority ordering. Show most impactful unlogged tags first:
       Sleep > Mood > Nutrition > Fitness > Body > Hydration > everything else.
     If user dismisses after 4 cards, the most important data is captured.
  3. "Log tomorrow" escape. After 5 cards, offer: "Save remaining for
     tomorrow?" This prevents the EOD from becoming a chore users avoid.
  4. Adaptive suppression. If a user consistently skips the same tag in EOD
     (skips 10+ times in a row), stop showing it in EOD and suggest: "You
     haven't logged [tag] in 30 days. Want to remove it from your tracked
     tags?" This keeps the EOD relevant over time.

Each card: Quick-input appropriate to the tag type (scale slider, checkbox,
  numeric input, text field).
Final card: AI-generated daily summary. Brief. Data-forward. Tone per tier.
  Example (Balanced tier): "Today: 1,850 of 2,000 cal target (92%). Protein
  at 134g of 150g. 72oz water. 45 min strength session. Mood: 4. Solid day."

Storage: Entries saved to respective dub.log.*.YYYY-MM-DD keys.

CHART PERFORMANCE STRATEGY (Per Expert 2 Audit, Severity: HIGH):
Without data aggregation and lazy rendering, the Trends tab will have
unacceptable performance on mid-range Android devices when displaying 365+
data points across multiple visible charts.

  Data Aggregation by Time Range:
    7d: Individual daily data points (no aggregation)
    30d: Individual daily data points (no aggregation)
    90d: Aggregate to weekly averages (13 data points)
    6mo: Aggregate to weekly averages (26 data points)
    1yr: Aggregate to weekly averages (52 data points)
    All Time: Aggregate to monthly averages (N data points)
  Charts ALWAYS read from pre-computed summary keys (dub.daily.summary,
  dub.weekly.summary, dub.monthly.summary), NEVER from raw log keys.
  Loading 365 individual AsyncStorage keys serially is unacceptable.

  Lazy Chart Rendering:
    The Trends tab chart grid uses FlatList (not ScrollView) with
    getItemLayout for fixed-height chart rows. Only charts currently in
    the viewport are rendered. Off-screen charts are unmounted.

  Thumbnail vs Detail Mode:
    Grid view: Sparkline-quality mini charts (simplified SVG, no
    interactivity, no labels, reduced point count). Tap to open.
    Detail view: Full interactive chart with axis labels, data point
    tooltips, pinch-to-zoom. Rendered one at a time in a modal or
    dedicated screen (app/trends/detail.tsx).

  GPU Rendering Fallback:
    For data-intensive visualizations (heatmaps, scatter plots with 500+
    points), consider @shopify/react-native-skia as alternative to SVG.
    Skia renders on the GPU and handles large datasets significantly
    better. Evaluate during Phase 16 build; if SVG performance is
    acceptable on a mid-range Android test device, skip Skia.


-- RECIPE RECOMMENDATION ENGINE --

AI Coach generates recipe recommendations based on:
  1. Remaining macros for the day (calories, protein, carbs, fat remaining
     against budget)
  2. Taste preferences (set during onboarding or in Settings: preferred
     cuisines, disliked ingredients, dietary restrictions)
  3. Observed patterns (frequently logged foods, time-of-day meal patterns)
  4. Available ingredient data (from FatSecret 19K+ recipes, USDA ingredient
     nutrition data, or AI-generated recipes with verified nutrition from
     USDA ingredient values)
  5. Substance goals (no alcohol in recipes if user has alcohol Quit goal)
  6. Flagged ingredients (avoid recipes containing user-flagged ingredients)

Taste Profiling (collected during onboarding or Settings):
  Preferred cuisines: American, Mexican, Italian, Chinese, Japanese, Indian,
    Thai, Mediterranean, Korean, Vietnamese, Middle Eastern, French, Ethiopian,
    Caribbean, Other
  Dietary restrictions: None, Vegetarian, Vegan, Pescatarian, Keto,
    Low-Carb, Gluten-Free, Dairy-Free, Nut-Free, Halal, Kosher
  Dislikes: Free-form ingredient list (e.g., "cilantro, anchovies, Brussels
    sprouts")

Every recipe card includes "Add to Instacart" button (deep link or API).
Recipe cards show: Name, photo (AI-generated or stock), calories, protein,
prep time, cook time, ingredient count, match score (how well it fits
remaining macros).

  Recipe Engine Prompt and Output Format (Per Expert 4 Audit, Severity: LOW):
    Store prompt template in ai/recipe_prompt.ts. Single Claude API call:

    Prompt:
      "Generate a recipe meeting these constraints:
      - Remaining macros: {cal}cal, {P}g protein, {C}g carbs, {F}g fat
      - Cuisines: {preferred list}
      - Restrictions: {dietary restrictions}
      - Avoid ingredients: {flagged + disliked list}
      - No alcohol in ingredients: {true if quit goal active}
      - Prep time: under 30 minutes preferred
      - Servings: {household size or 1}
      Return as JSON with: name, description, prep_time_min, cook_time_min,
      servings, ingredients [{name, amount, unit, calories, protein_g}],
      instructions [{step_number, text}], total_nutrition {cal, protein_g,
      carbs_g, fat_g}, macro_match_pct.
      If no recipe can meet all constraints within 10%, suggest the closest
      match and explain which constraint was relaxed in a 'relaxed_constraint'
      field."

    Output: 400-700 tokens. Fits within max_tokens=1024 with headroom.
    UI: Recipe card with editable serving count (nutrition scales linearly).

  CRITICAL -- COACH/MARKETPLACE SEPARATION (Per Expert 3 Audit, Severity: MEDIUM):
    The AI Coach MUST NEVER include affiliate links, product recommendations,
    or purchase CTAs within its chat responses. Blending health insights with
    commercial recommendations is the exact pattern the FTC has targeted.
    If a user asks Coach "what Vitamin D supplement should I take?", Coach
    provides general guidance (dosage, form, timing) but does NOT link to a
    specific product. Instead, Coach may say: "Check the Marketplace tab for
    Vitamin D options that fit your profile."
    Marketplace recommendations appear ONLY in the Marketplace section of the
    app, visually distinct from Coach, with clear "Advertisement" or
    "Sponsored" framing. The Coach tab and the Marketplace tab are separate
    experiences. They do not cross-contaminate.
    The Coach's data analysis code and the marketplace's product trigger code
    must NEVER share the same code path. Coach decides insights based on
    health value; marketplace decides products based on behavior triggers.
    They do not influence each other architecturally.
    [LEGAL COUNSEL REQUIRED -- this separation design should be reviewed by
    FTC compliance counsel before launch.]

  MARKETPLACE ETHICS (Per Expert 5 Audit, Severity: MEDIUM):
    1. Time delay on deficit-based triggers: If a user logs a health data point
       that could trigger a product recommendation (e.g., low Vitamin D in
       bloodwork), the product recommendation does NOT appear the same day.
       Wait 7 days. If the pattern persists (multiple low values or Coach
       mentions the deficiency), then surface the product. This prevents the
       perception of "I told the app about a problem and it immediately tried
       to sell me something."
    2. "Why am I seeing this?" button on every product card. Tapping shows the
       exact trigger: "You were shown this because you logged low Vitamin D in
       your last 2 bloodwork entries." Full transparency defuses suspicion.
    3. Architectural separation: Coach data analysis and marketplace product
       triggers run on separate code paths. The marketplace reads from
       dub.coach.patterns (output of pattern engine) but does NOT call the
       pattern engine or Coach context builder directly.

  FTC Disclosure: Every product card displays: "This is an affiliate link.
  DUB_AI may earn a commission if you purchase through this link." Per FTC
  Endorsement Guides 16 CFR Part 255 (effective July 26, 2023). Disclosure
  must be clear, conspicuous, and proximate to the product recommendation.
  Disclosure text appears within the visible area of the product card BEFORE
  any product description, price, or buy button (i.e., visible without
  scrolling within the card).


-- INFLUENCER MARKETPLACE --

  Open Application Model: Influencers apply via in-app or web form.
  Approval: Manual review (Phase 2+, initially manual by DUB_AI team).

  How It Works:
    1. Influencer negotiates their own vendor/brand deals (DUB_AI is not

    2. Influencer brings products into DUB_AI storefront with their own

    3. DUB_AI takes a percentage of the influencer's commission on every sale

       before DUB_AI can demonstrate conversion rates. Launch at 15-20% to

       The primary value of the influencer marketplace at launch is as a
       USER ACQUISITION channel (influencers promote DUB_AI to their
       audiences) more than as a revenue channel.
    4. Each influencer gets: unique partner code, branded storefront page
       within DUB_AI, self-selected niche/category alignment
    5. Sales tracked via affiliate link click-through attribution

  FTC Dual Disclosure (required on every influencer product card):
    Disclosure 1: "[Influencer Name] has a paid relationship with [Brand].
      They may earn a commission on purchases."
    Disclosure 2: "DUB_AI earns a portion of this commission."

    No unverified health claims
    No before/after photos with misleading timelines
    No income claims

    Consequences (progressive):
      First violation: Written warning with specific correction required
      Second violation: 30-day suspension from marketplace
      Third violation: Permanent removal, affiliate codes deactivated
    Monitoring cadence: Quarterly for top-10 by revenue; ad-hoc for
      user-reported violations (add "Report this influencer" link on
      influencer storefront pages).
    Automated future (v2+): Keyword scanning of influencer social posts
      containing DUB_AI affiliate codes for prohibited health claim terms.

SECTION 13A: USER ACQUISITION, GROWTH MODEL, AND UNIT ECONOMICS
================================================================================

Per Expert 6 Monetization Audit (Severity: HIGH): The spec describes
monetization in detail but says NOTHING about how users will find and download
the app. This is the most significant strategic gap. Additionally, the BYOK
model and no-social policy together create a growth ceiling that must be
acknowledged and modeled explicitly.

USER ACQUISITION CHANNELS:

  1. App Store Optimization (ASO): MODERATE potential. "Calorie tracker" and
     "fitness tracker" are highly competitive keywords dominated by
     MyFitnessPal, Lose It!, and Cronometer. DUB_AI differentiation (AI coach,
     no social, clinical depth, data privacy, 22 health dimensions) must be
     clearly communicated in listing title, subtitle, and screenshots.

  2. Content Marketing / SEO: HIGH potential. The spec's clinical depth
     (verified citations, evidence-based formulas, 22 health dimensions) is
     excellent content material. Blog posts on aimhigherbook.com, comparison
     articles ("DUB_AI vs MyFitnessPal"), YouTube walkthroughs, and the book
     "Aim Higher" can drive organic discovery. This is the PRIMARY organic
     channel.

  3. Influencer Partnerships: MEDIUM potential. The influencer marketplace is
     designed for this. However, "no social features" limits virality -- there
     is no "share my progress" or "invite a friend" mechanism.

  4. B2B Partnerships (later stage): HIGH potential. MAHA ELEVATE Cohort 2,
     DPC practices, employer wellness programs. Each partnership brings a
     captive audience. This is the long-term growth engine.

  5. Professional Referral: MEDIUM potential (slow-burn, high-quality). RDs,
     personal trainers, and DPC physicians who see the Health Report PDF could
     recommend DUB_AI to clients/patients.

  "NO SOCIAL" GROWTH IMPACT:
    DUB_AI's Rule 2 (no sharing, no friends, no feed) is a deliberate design
    choice that constrains growth. Comparable apps derive 10-20% of organic
    installs from social features (friend invitations, shared diaries, group
    challenges). DUB_AI must rely on product quality, content marketing, and
    B2B distribution instead. Model growth at 20-30% of comparable social-
    enabled apps' organic growth rate.

  "BYOK" ADOPTION BARRIER:
    The Bring Your Own API Key model is a significant friction point. The
    typical wellness app user does not know what an API key is. Estimated
    adoption: of users who install DUB_AI, 30-50% will complete API key setup.
    The remaining 50-70% will use the app as a tracker without AI Coach.

TWO USER SEGMENTS (Per Expert 6 Audit, Severity: MEDIUM):
  The spec must explicitly design for BOTH segments:

  Segment 1 -- "Full DUB_AI" (API key + Coach):
    Power users, technically savvy, high engagement. Estimated 30-50% of
    installs. These users access all features including AI Coach, pattern
    recognition, recipe recommendations, and proactive insights.

  Segment 2 -- "Tracker-Only" (no API key):
    Mainstream users, no AI Coach. Estimated 50-70% of installs. These users
    get: food logging, body metrics, sleep/mood tracking, exercise logging,
    trends/charts, EOD questionnaire, all manual features. The app must NOT
    feel broken without an API key. Coach tab shows: "Set up AI Coach" with
    a clear tutorial link, plus a preview of what Coach can do. Dashboard,
    Log, Trends, and Settings tabs are fully functional without a key.

  Design principle: Every feature that does NOT require the Anthropic API
  must work perfectly for Tracker-Only users. The Coach is an enhancement,
  not a gate.

  FUTURE CONSIDERATION (Roadmap, not v1): If DUB_AI reaches 50K+ MAU,
  consider a managed API tier: DUB_AI provides a proxy API key funded by a
  subscription ($5-8/month "DUB_AI Pro"). This unlocks the mass market while
  preserving BYOK for power users.

PROJECTED GROWTH PATH (Expert 6, conservative estimate):
  Months 1-3: 500-2,000 users (founder network, early content, book launch)
  Months 4-6: 2,000-5,000 users (ASO, blog content, initial influencer
    partnerships)
  Months 7-12: 5,000-15,000 users (content SEO compounding, influencer
    marketplace growth, professional referrals beginning)
  Year 2: 15,000-50,000 users (B2B partnerships, MAHA ELEVATE Cohort 2 if
    secured, managed API tier consideration)
  This is 10-20x slower than a comparable app with social features and a free
  AI tier. The business plan must be viable at these user counts.

UNIT ECONOMICS (Per Expert 6 Audit, Severity: LOW):

  Cost per user (v1, BYOK model):
    AI inference: $0 (user pays Anthropic directly via their API key)
    Food database (USDA, Open Food Facts): $0 (free / public domain)
    Food database (FatSecret free tier): $0 (within 10K calls/day)
    Backend hosting: $0 (v1 has no backend; all data local)
    App Store fees: $99/year (Apple) + $25 one-time (Google) = negligible
    Total COGS per user: ~$0 (near-zero marginal cost)

  Revenue per user (affiliate model):
    Per Expert 6 analysis: ~$1.44 per 1,000 MAU per month from affiliates.
    This is insufficient as sole revenue but viable at near-zero COGS.

  Breakeven: With ~$500/month fixed costs (domain, email, basic tooling),

  STATISTICAL METHOD (Per Expert 4 Audit, Severity: HIGH):
  Do NOT use Pearson/Spearman correlation with small samples. Use tiered:

    Tier 1 -- Threshold Counting (7+ data points):
      Simple occurrence counting. "On X of Y days where condition A was true,
      outcome B occurred." Example: "On 5 of 7 days where you drank 64+ oz
      water, your mood was 4+." No statistical claims. Just counts.
      Applicable to: simple threshold patterns, ingredient frequency.

    Tier 2 -- Rolling Average Comparison (14+ data points):
      Compare 7-day averages of outcome variable split by condition. Report
      only if difference exceeds 15% AND occurs in at least 3 of 4 weeks.
      Applicable to: time-of-day correlations, hydration-mood, substance-
      performance.

      typical in health data). Report only if rho >= 0.4 and p < 0.05.
      Applicable to: food-outcome correlations, cross-tag correlations,
      cycle-specific patterns.

SECTION 16: OFFLINE STRATEGY
================================================================================

All logging operations must work offline. Implementation:

1. Every log entry is saved to AsyncStorage immediately (local-first).
2. API calls (food search, Coach chat, weather) are attempted and fail
   gracefully if offline.
3. Failed API-dependent operations are queued in dub.offline.queue with
   operation type, payload, and local timestamp.
4. On reconnect (detected via NetInfo or similar), queue is processed FIFO.
5. Conflict resolution: Local timestamp wins. User's device is the source
   of truth for when events occurred.
6. Food cache (last 500 items) enables offline food logging from previously
   used foods.

This means every core logging feature works without internet. Only AI Coach
chat and food API search require connectivity.

SECTION 17: SECURITY ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

Even in v1 (AsyncStorage, no backend), implement these security measures:

1. API Key Storage: User's Anthropic API key stored via expo-secure-store,
   which provides hardware-backed secure storage on both platforms:
     iOS: Keychain (hardware-backed, resistant to extraction even on
       jailbroken devices)
     Android: EncryptedSharedPreferences backed by Android Keystore
       (hardware-backed on devices with Trusted Execution Environment)
   Do NOT store API keys in AsyncStorage, even encrypted. Do NOT derive
   encryption keys from device identifiers (ANDROID_ID etc.) -- these are
   readable by any app on rooted devices.
   Per Expert 2 Mobile Architecture Audit finding (Severity: HIGH).
   AsyncStorage key dub.apikey is REMOVED. API key lives exclusively in
   expo-secure-store under key "dub_anthropic_api_key".

2. Health Data Encryption at Rest (HIPAA Prep): For Strategic Plays 1-3,
   health data will require AES-256 encryption at rest. The encryption key
   must be derived from a user-provided PIN/password using PBKDF2 (minimum
   100,000 iterations, SHA-256), NOT from a device identifier. This is a v2
   requirement but encryption.ts should be architected for it now. Use
   react-native-aes-crypto (NOT expo-crypto, which only provides hashing
   and random bytes -- it does not support AES encryption/decryption).
   Per Expert 2 Audit finding (Severity: MEDIUM).

3. TLS 1.3: All API calls use HTTPS (enforced by React Native fetch).

4. No PII in Logs: Console.log statements never include user health data,
   API keys, or PII. Debug logging disabled in production builds.

5. Photo Privacy: Progress photos stored as local file URIs only. Never
   uploaded. Never included in any export unless user explicitly selects.

6. Therapy Note Isolation: TherapyEntry.notes field is flagged in the type
   system. Code review checklist: ensure this field is never included in
   Coach context, Health Report, data export (except full encrypted backup),
   or any external transmission.

7. Data Deletion: Settings > Delete My Data removes all AsyncStorage keys,
   all expo-secure-store keys, all cached files, all photos. Confirmation
   dialog with explicit "I understand this cannot be undone" checkbox.

8. Biometric Lock (Future): Stub for FaceID/TouchID app lock. Not implemented
   in v1 but architecture should not preclude it.

9. AsyncStorage Key Size Monitoring: The storage wrapper (src/utils/storage.ts)
   must log the byte size of every value on write and emit a warning when any
   single key exceeds 1 MB (50% of the Android CursorWindow 2 MB limit).
   At 1.5 MB (75% of limit), block the write and trigger a data compaction
   or key-splitting operation. The CursorWindow crash is silent and
   unrecoverable -- preventing it is critical.
   Per Expert 2 Audit finding (Severity: HIGH).
   Monitoring targets:
     dub.coach.history -- enforce 200 message cap; at ~2KB/message pair this
       stays under 400 KB (SAFE)
     dub.food.cache -- enforce 500 item cap with LRU eviction (SAFE)
     dub.weekly.summary.* and dub.monthly.summary.* -- store AGGREGATED
       STATISTICS ONLY (averages, totals, counts), NOT full daily data arrays.
       A monthly summary must be a compact statistical object, not a copy of
       30 days of raw log data.

SECTION 17A: BREACH NOTIFICATION PROTOCOL
================================================================================

Per Expert 3 Privacy Audit (Severity: CRITICAL). The FTC Health Breach
Notification Rule (16 CFR Part 318, final rule published May 30, 2024,
effective July 29, 2024) requires vendors of personal health records to
notify individuals and the FTC of breaches. The FTC has actively enforced
this rule against consumer health apps (GoodRx: $1.5M penalty; Premom:
settlement). DUB_AI MUST have a breach notification plan before launch.
[LEGAL COUNSEL REQUIRED]

DEFINITION OF BREACH EVENTS FOR DUB_AI:
  A "breach of security" under the FTC HBN Rule means unauthorized
  acquisition of identifiable health data. For DUB_AI, breach events include:
    1. Unauthorized transmission of health data to any endpoint other than
       the Anthropic Messages API (the only authorized third-party data flow)
    2. Unintended inclusion of therapy notes in Coach context, Health Report,
       data export, or any external transmission
    3. API key exposure in application logs, crash reports, or error messages
    4. Unencrypted health data in a data export file that is subsequently
       exposed (e.g., user shares unencrypted JSON export to cloud storage)
    5. A vulnerability in the app that permits another app on the device to
       read DUB_AI's AsyncStorage database
    6. Any third-party SDK or library transmitting DUB_AI health data without
       user consent (e.g., analytics SDK capturing screen content)

DETECTION MECHANISMS (v1, local-first):
  1. Code-level safeguards: Static analysis rules to prevent therapy notes
     from appearing in non-isolated code paths. Enforce via TypeScript type
     system (TherapyEntry.notes typed as PrivateField<string> that cannot be
     passed to Coach context builder or export functions).
  2. Runtime transmission audit: Log all outbound network requests in debug
     builds. Verify that only Anthropic API and approved food/weather APIs
     receive data. No health data in request URLs or query parameters.
  3. Build-time audit: Pre-release checklist verifying no PII in console.log,
     no therapy notes in context builder, no API keys in error reporting.
  4. Crash reporting exclusion: If a crash reporting service is added (e.g.,
     Sentry), configure data scrubbing to exclude all health data fields,
     API keys, and user-entered text from crash reports.

NOTIFICATION WORKFLOW:
  If a breach is discovered:
    1. Document: Date of discovery, nature of breach, data types involved,
       estimated number of affected users, remediation steps taken
    2. Notify affected individuals within 60 calendar days of discovery via:
       in-app notification (if user can still access app), email (if collected),
       and prominent notice on DUB_AI website
    3. Notify the FTC: If 500+ individuals affected, notify within 60 days.

SECTION 17B: THIRD-PARTY DATA PROCESSING DISCLOSURE
================================================================================

Per Expert 3 Privacy Audit (Severity: HIGH). Under the 2024 FTC HBN Rule
amendments, sharing user health data with a third-party API provider could
constitute a "breach" if not disclosed and consented to. The Premom
enforcement action specifically targeted undisclosed sharing of health data.
[LEGAL COUNSEL REQUIRED]

ANTHROPIC API DATA FLOW:
  The following user health data is transmitted to Anthropic's servers via the
  Messages API when the user interacts with Coach DUB:
    - User-typed messages (which may contain any health information the user
      chooses to share, including therapy details)
    - System prompt containing: profile summary (name, age, sex, height,
      weight), today's logged data (calories, macros, water, exercise, mood,
      sleep, substances, recovery score), 7-day rolling statistics, active
      injuries, latest bloodwork values, cycle phase, supplement flags,
      sobriety goal status
    - Photo+AI food estimation: meal photos sent for analysis
    - NLP food parsing: natural language food descriptions

  This data flow MUST be:
    1. Disclosed in the privacy policy with specificity: "When you use the AI
       Coach feature, your health data and messages are transmitted to
       Anthropic, PBC for processing. Anthropic processes this data to
       generate Coach responses."
    2. Consented to by the user BEFORE first Coach interaction. The consent
       screen must appear when the user first opens the Coach tab or enters
       their API key, whichever comes first.
    3. Reviewed against Anthropic's Terms of Service and Data Processing
       Agreement to confirm Anthropic does not retain or train on user health
       data transmitted via the API. [LEGAL COUNSEL REQUIRED]

  NOTE ON "BRING YOUR OWN KEY" MODEL: The user provides their own Anthropic
  API key. This may shift some data processing responsibility to the user
  (they are authorizing their own API access), but this is untested legal
  territory. Do NOT rely on this as a compliance defense without legal
  counsel review.

  Coach Disclaimer (added to Coach tab consent flow and persistent footer):
    "Information you type in Coach messages is processed by a third-party AI
    service (Anthropic). Do not share therapy details, passwords, financial
    account numbers, or other sensitive information in Coach messages if you
    want them to remain private. Coach messages are not covered by therapist-
    patient privilege."
